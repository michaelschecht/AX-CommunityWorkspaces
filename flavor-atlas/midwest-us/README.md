# Midwest United States Cuisine

## Regional Overview
Heartland American cooking featuring comfort food classics, farm-to-table traditions, and dishes that reflect the agricultural abundance of the Great Plains and Great Lakes regions. This collection celebrates the immigrant influences (German, Scandinavian, Polish) and American innovation that define Midwestern cuisine.

## Cuisine Characteristics
- Hearty, filling portions emphasizing meat, potatoes, and dairy
- Casserole culture and one-dish meals
- Preservation techniques: pickling, canning, smoking
- Strong German, Polish, and Scandinavian influences
- Emphasis on seasonal produce and local ingredients
- Classic American comfort foods

## Representative Dishes
- Chicago-style deep dish pizza
- Hot dishes and casseroles
- Bratwurst and sausages
- Cheese curds and dairy specialties
- Apple pie and fruit desserts
- Corn-based dishes
- Pot roasts and slow-cooked meats

## Recipe Guidelines
All recipes in this folder must follow the [FlavorAtlas_Recipe_Template.md](../FlavorAtlas_Recipe_Template.md) format and include:
- Regional context and cultural significance
- Scaling notes for family gatherings
- Make-ahead and storage instructions
- Seasonal variations when applicable
