# Mexican Cuisine

## Regional Overview
One of the world's oldest and most influential cuisines, Mexican cooking combines indigenous Mesoamerican traditions with Spanish colonial influences. Each region of Mexico has distinct flavors, techniques, and signature dishes, from Oaxacan moles to Yucatecan citrus-forward preparations.

## Cuisine Characteristics
- Corn, beans, and chiles as foundational ingredients
- Complex sauce preparations (moles, salsas, adobos)
- Ancient techniques: nixtamalization, stone grinding
- Regional diversity: coastal vs. highland vs. northern
- Fresh herbs: cilantro, epazote, Mexican oregano
- Dried chiles creating depth and complexity
- Handmade tortillas and masa-based dishes

## Representative Dishes
- Tacos (countless regional varieties)
- Mole (negro, poblano, verde, amarillo)
- Tamales
- Pozole and menudo
- Enchiladas and chilaquiles
- Cochinita pibil
- Fresh salsas and guacamole
