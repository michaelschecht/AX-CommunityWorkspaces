# South Asian Cuisine

## Regional Overview
South Asian cuisine encompasses the diverse culinary traditions of India (with vast regional differences), Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, and surrounding regions. Characterized by complex spice blends, layered flavors, and vegetarian traditions alongside rich meat preparations.

## Cuisine Characteristics
- Complex spice blends: garam masala, curry powders, regional masalas
- Layering and tempering (tadka) techniques
- Yogurt, ghee, and coconut milk as bases
- Bread traditions: naan, roti, paratha, dosa
- Rice varieties: basmati, sona masoori, red rice
- Legumes and lentils (dal) as protein sources
- Regional diversity: North vs. South, East vs. West

## Representative Dishes
- North Indian: Butter chicken, tikka masala, biryani, samosas
- South Indian: Dosa, idli, sambar, chettinad curries
- Pakistani: Nihari, karahi, haleem
- Sri Lankan: Rice and curry, hoppers, kottu
- Bengali: Fish curries, puchka, mishti
- Punjabi: Sarson ka saag, chole, tandoori dishes
