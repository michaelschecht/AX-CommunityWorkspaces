# East Asian Cuisine

## Regional Overview
East Asian cuisine includes the culinary traditions of China (all regions), Japan, Korea, and Vietnam. Each has distinct flavors, techniques, and philosophies, from Chinese wok cooking to Japanese kaiseki precision to Korean fermentation traditions to Vietnamese herb-forward freshness.

## Cuisine Characteristics
- Rice and noodles as staples
- Soy sauce, miso, and fermented ingredients for umami
- Wok cooking and high-heat stir-frying
- Steaming, braising, and slow-cooking techniques
- Balance and harmony in flavor and presentation
- Regional spice profiles: Sichuan heat, Korean gochugaru, Vietnamese aromatics
- Pickled and fermented vegetables

## Representative Dishes
- Chinese: Kung pao chicken, mapo tofu, dumplings, Peking duck
- Japanese: Ramen, sushi, tempura, teriyaki, tonkatsu
- Korean: Kimchi, bulgogi, bibimbap, Korean fried chicken
- Vietnamese: Pho, banh mi, spring rolls, bun cha
