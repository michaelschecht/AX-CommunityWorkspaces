# New Orleans Cuisine

## Regional Overview
The unique fusion of Creole and Cajun cooking traditions born in Louisiana, combining French, Spanish, West African, Native American, and Caribbean influences. New Orleans cuisine is celebrated for its bold flavors, complex spice blends, and soul-satisfying comfort.

## Cuisine Characteristics
- The "Holy Trinity": celery, bell peppers, onions
- Roux-based sauces and gravies
- Rice as a foundation
- Bold seasoning with Creole and Cajun spice blends
- Seafood prominence: crawfish, shrimp, oysters, crab
- African, French, and Spanish cooking techniques
- One-pot cooking traditions

## Representative Dishes
- Gumbo (seafood, chicken & sausage, or z'herbes)
- Jambalaya
- Red beans and rice
- Po' boys and muffulettas
- Étouffée (crawfish or shrimp)
- Beignets and pralines
- Dirty rice and boudin
